Nikolai Grigoriev

Process

This is a symbiotic artwork. The painting is not composed by the artist directly — it is generated from the artist's own physiological data, recorded continuously by a wearable biometric sensor (Oura Ring). The body is both the subject and the instrument. This is not a fixed picture but a living loop: each night the body writes the next day, the work syncs it the following morning, and the canvas opens on the most recent self. The image on screen is only the current frame of an ongoing relationship.

Fourteen physiological measurements are extracted from each 24-hour cycle, normalized, and mapped to independent visual parameters. The same body-state always produces the same painting — the process is deterministic; the seed is the body, not the date.

Silence

The loop can break. When the signal stops — the ring removed, the body still — the work does not pretend. The last day freezes, and a void grows into the canvas in proportion to the silence. A gap is not smoothed over; it is the most honest image the work can hold. The painting is built to register an ending, not to outlive one.

Source Signal

The sensor tracks the body during sleep and activity. From each day, the system reads: readiness score, sleep score, heart rate variability, resting heart rate, deep and REM sleep percentage, sleep efficiency, onset latency, restless periods, body temperature deviation, and workout intensity and frequency.

Mood Index

Three core metrics — readiness, sleep quality, and HRV — are averaged into a single value between 0 and 1. This index drives the whole composition: it shifts the boundary between the two palettes across the canvas, sets stroke density and layer count, and tilts the painting's axis. A high value opens the field to the bright palette (warm, airy); a low value sinks it into deep wine, bordeaux, and near-black. The boundary is spatial, not random — it moves with the body across the surface.

Construction

Each painting is built in five sequential passes using simulated oil brushes. Broad underpainting sweeps establish the tonal field. Curved gestural strokes build up in layers — two on well-rested days, six on exhausted ones. Drip trails and micro-splatters accumulate from physical activity. Thick impasto accents mark quick sleep onset. On restless nights, a final scatter of fine marks appears at the edges.
