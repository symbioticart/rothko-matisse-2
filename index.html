Nikolai Grigoriev

Process

This is a symbiotic artwork. The painting is not composed by the artist directly — it is generated from the artist's own physiological data, recorded continuously by a wearable biometric sensor (Oura Ring). The body is both the subject and the instrument. This is not a fixed picture but a living loop: each night the body writes the next day, the work syncs it the following morning, and the canvas opens on the most recent self. The image on screen is only the current frame of an ongoing relationship.

Fourteen physiological measurements are extracted from each 24-hour cycle, normalized, and mapped to independent visual parameters. The same body-state always produces the same painting — the process is deterministic; the seed is the body, not the date.

Source Signal

The sensor tracks the body during sleep and activity. From each day, the system reads: readiness score, sleep score, heart rate variability, resting heart rate, deep and REM sleep percentage, sleep efficiency, onset latency, restless periods, body temperature deviation, and workout intensity and frequency.

Mood Index

Three core metrics — readiness, sleep quality, and HRV — are averaged into a single value between 0 and 1. This index sets the painting's overall key: it moves the boundary between the two palettes across the surface, governs how dense the field becomes, and tilts the whole composition's axis. A high value opens the field to the bright palette (warm, airy); a low value sinks it into deep wine, bordeaux, and near-black. The boundary is spatial, not random — it travels with the body across the canvas.

Three Registers

The body never speaks in one voice. At any moment the deep, slow systems and the fast, surface ones run at once, and the painting keeps them as three distinct scales of mark. The foundation — a few broad grounds — carries the recovered, parasympathetic body: deep sleep and heart-rate variability. The modulation — the curved middle gestures — is the waking self: readiness and physical activity. The tremor — a swarm of small, sharp marks — is the agitated surface: restlessness, breathing rate, an elevated resting pulse. A calm, recovered day is carried by large, confident strokes; a depleted, stressed one fragments into many small nervous ones.

Variance is HRV

Within every register the marks differ in length and width. The width of that spread is heart-rate variability itself: an adaptive, high-HRV day scatters marks across a wide range of sizes; a rigid, low-HRV day collapses them toward a single anxious scale. The literal variability of the heart becomes the visual variability of the hand.

Construction

Each painting is built in layered passes with simulated oil brushes. The foundation register is laid first as broad grounds, then the modulation register builds curved gestures over it, then the tremor swarm settles across the surface. Physical activity is thrown on as drip trails and splatters; quick sleep onset leaves a few thick, immediate impasto accents at the field's peaks. Every mark is held inside the frame, and a calculated margin is always left bare — the painting breathes.
